How Much Does It Cost to Live in Thailand as a Teacher?

A complete breakdown of living costs—housing, food, transport, and more.

December 2024 · 7 min read

One of the biggest questions future teachers ask is: "How much money will I actually spend each month in Thailand?"

The good news? Thailand is one of the most affordable countries to live in—especially for teachers. With a low cost of living, you can enjoy daily comfort, travel often, and still save money each month.

Here's a full breakdown of what you can expect.

Housing Costs (with MediaKids Support)

MediaKids provides a monthly housing allowance and helps teachers find accommodation close to their school.

Most teachers spend:

  • 3,000–6,000 THB per month for a private studio
  • Often includes A/C, bathroom, balcony
  • Kitchens are rare (most people eat out—it's cheaper!)

If your rent is lower, you save. If it's slightly higher, you can easily cover the difference.

Food Costs

Thailand is famous for delicious and affordable food.

Typical prices:

  • Street food meal: 40–70 THB
  • Noodles or rice dishes: 50–70 THB
  • Coffee: 30–60 THB
  • Sit-down restaurant: 80–150 THB

Eating out is often cheaper than cooking at home.

Transportation Costs

Transport in Thailand is extremely budget-friendly.

Average prices:

  • Local bus: 10–20 THB
  • Motorbike taxi: 20–60 THB
  • Taxi starting fare: 40 THB
  • Monthly motorbike rental: 1,500–4,000 THB
  • Train or bus to another city: 300–800 THB

Most teachers choose a motorbike or live close enough to walk.

Phone & Internet

Thailand has reliable phone service and fast 4G/5G.

Monthly cost:

  • 200–400 THB for unlimited data
  • Home WiFi: 300–600 THB (optional — many teachers use mobile data only)

Lifestyle & Entertainment

Teachers can enjoy a fun social life without overspending.

Examples:

  • Movie ticket: 100–200 THB
  • Gym membership: 500–1,200 THB
  • Thai massage: 250–350 THB per hour
  • Weekend trip: 800–2,000 THB (including transport + food)

Thailand offers an amazing lifestyle at a fraction of Western costs.

Estimated Monthly Budget for Teachers

Here's a realistic sample monthly breakdown:

Expense Estimated Cost
Rent 3,000–6,000 THB
Food 3,000–6,000 THB
Transport 500–1,500 THB
Phone/Data 200–400 THB
Entertainment 2,000–4,000 THB
Total 8,700–17,900 THB / month

This leaves teachers with plenty of money left over each month.

Why Thailand Is One of the Most Affordable TEFL Destinations

Compared to Japan, Korea, Vietnam, or China, Thailand offers:

  • Lower startup costs
  • Lower monthly expenses
  • Easier work-life balance
  • A soft landing for first-time teachers

Most teachers say they enjoy a lifestyle they could never afford back home.

Final Thoughts

Thailand offers a comfortable, enjoyable lifestyle with the ability to live well—and save money. With MediaKids' support, housing assistance, and guidance, teachers start their journey with confidence.
